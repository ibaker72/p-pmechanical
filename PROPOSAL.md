## SEO — being found on Google

This isn't bolted on. SEO is baked into the foundation:

- **Per-city pages** so you rank for "boiler repair Clifton," "AC installation Passaic," "heating contractor Montclair," etc. — instead of competing for one impossible "near me" keyword
- **Structured data** (schema.org markup) so Google understands you're a local HVAC business, your service area, your hours, and your services — this is what powers those rich Google Maps boxes
- **Automatic sitemap** regenerated on every deploy — Google always sees fresh content
- **Blog content engine** — easy to add seasonal articles ("When to turn the heat on in NJ," "Boiler vs. furnace") that pull in homeowners who are just starting their research
- **Reading-time, meta descriptions, OpenGraph images, robots.txt** — every technical box is checked
- **Core Web Vitals optimized** — Google now rewards sites that load instantly, and this one does

### GEO — being found by ChatGPT, Gemini, and Google AI

This is where most contractor websites are about to fall behind, and where yours will pull ahead.

When someone asks an AI assistant "who should I call for a boiler repair in Clifton NJ?", the AI doesn't read websites the way Google does. It looks for **structured, machine-readable information** about your business.

Your site ships with:

- **`llms.txt` and `llms-full.txt`** — the new emerging standard that tells AI models exactly who you are, what you do, where you serve, and how to contact you
- **Agent-friendly manifest** — so AI shopping assistants and voice agents (Siri, Alexa, ChatGPT) can pull accurate info about your services and hours
- **Clean, semantic HTML** that AI crawlers love
- **FAQ content written in question/answer format** — exactly what AI engines quote in their answers

Result: when the AI revolution in local search finishes playing out (it's happening _now_), you're already in the answer.
